Getting started

Three things to do before The Content Tracker starts working for you.

1
Connect Google

Go to Settings and click Connect Google. Sign in with the Google account that has access to your Search Console and Analytics properties. This grants The Content Tracker read-only access — it never modifies anything in your Google account.

2
Add your sites

Navigate to Sites and add each website you want to track. Enter the exact GSC property string — you can find this in the Search Console property dropdown. URL-prefix properties look like https://www.example.com/ and domain properties look like sc-domain:example.com. The Content Tracker will pull performance data for each site separately and show them in one combined dashboard.

3
Select your Analytics property

Go to the Analytics dashboard. After connecting Google, a dropdown will show your GA4 properties. Select the one that matches the site you want to track pageviews for. If you manage multiple sites, the Multi-Site Dashboard aggregates Search Console data across all of them automatically.

Good to know

The Expert Profile grows with every transcript you process. After 3-5 transcripts, the Writing Kit becomes genuinely useful — it captures the expert's real voice, not a generic summary.

Search Console data has a 2-3 day delay from Google's side. Don't expect yesterday's numbers to appear immediately.

When you publish an article, come back and mark it as published on the content page. This closes the loop — The Content Tracker will then pull performance data for that specific URL and show you exactly how it performs in search.

All sites in your account should be connected to the same Google account. Multi-account support for sites across different Google accounts is planned for a future version.
